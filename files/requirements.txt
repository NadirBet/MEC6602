# CFD Solver Requirements Specification

## 1. Computation of mesh metrics

### R1 – Cell area (2D "volume")
The code must calculate the area of a 2D cell using the formula:

volume = 0.5 * | a × b |

(where "volume" in 2D is an area).

### R2 – Face normal
The normal vector of a face is obtained as the transpose (90° rotation) of the edge vector.

The code must store the normalized normal vector (norm = 1).

### R3 – Face area
The code must also store the face surface (in 2D: the face length).

### R4 – Consistent normal orientation
The direction of normal vectors must be consistent across the entire mesh.

For each face, the code must identify a "left" cell and a "right" cell relative to that face (topological sense).

The face normal must be defined from "left" to "right".

"Left" and "right" are to be understood topologically (according to mesh ordering), not geometrically.

## 2. Structured Plot3D file format

### R5 – Input format
The input mesh is given in Plot3D format and must be read in this order:

- An index indicating the number of blocks m
- For each of the m blocks: a triplet of indices ni, nj (and k if in the file)
- A list of coordinates in order:
  - all x of block 1 (order i, j, k)
  - all y of block 1 (order i, j, k)
  - then same for block 2, etc.

### R6 – Size
The provided meshes range from 9×9 to 2049×2049.

### R7 – Ghost layers
After reading the Plot3D mesh, the code must add 2 layers of ghost cells for boundary condition implementation.

## 3. Convective fluxes discretization

### R8 – Scheme type
The numerical scheme used for convective fluxes must be either upwinded or stabilized by adding numerical dissipation.

### R9 – Flux calculation location
To avoid truncation error problems, the convective flux must be calculated at the face and then added to the residual of the two adjacent cells.

If the flux is calculated in a loop over cells, it will be counted twice.

### R10 – Normal direction
When adding the flux to the left cell and right cell, the direction of the stored normal vector (defined "left → right" according to previous slides) must be taken into account.

What exits the left cell must enter the right cell.

Therefore, for one of the two cells, the flux must be inverted.

## 4. Centered fluxes discretization

Consider the face between state 0 and state 1, with normal $\vec{n}_{01}$ and length $\Delta S_{01}$.

### Method #1 (interior)
$$(\vec{F}_c \Delta S)_{01} \approx \frac{1}{2}\left[ \vec{F}_c(\vec{W}_0, \vec{n}_{01}) + \vec{F}_c(\vec{W}_1, \vec{n}_{01}) \right] \Delta S_{01}$$

### Method #2 (physical boundary)
$$(\vec{F}_c \Delta S)_{01} \approx \vec{F}_c(\vec{W}_{01}, \vec{n}_{01}) \Delta S_{01}, \quad \vec{W}_{01} = \frac{1}{2}(\vec{W}_0 + \vec{W}_1)$$

### R11 – Method choice
The two methods are not equivalent.

- Method #1 must be used for interior faces
- Method #2 must be used for physical boundaries (and not for internal connectivities)

## 5. Central scheme with scalar dissipation

### R12 – Convective flux (central)
On face $(i+\frac{1}{2}, j, k)$, the convective flux must be evaluated as:

$$(\vec{F}_c \Delta S)_{i+1/2,j,k} \approx \vec{F}_c(\tilde{\vec{W}}_{i+1/2,j,k}, \vec{n}_{i+1/2,j,k}) \Delta S_{i+1/2,j,k}$$

"See previous slide and replace by method 1" - the average of the two states on either side of the face must be done according to method 1 from the previous slide for interior faces.

### R13 – Centered state
The centered state used in the flux is:

$$\tilde{\vec{W}}_{i+1/2,j,k} = \frac{1}{2}(\vec{W}_{i,j,k} + \vec{W}_{i+1,j,k})$$

### R14 – Scalar artificial dissipation
An artificial dissipation term is added on the same face:

$$\tilde{D}_{i+1/2} = \hat{\Lambda}_{i+1/2}^S \left[\epsilon_{i+1/2}^{(2)}(\vec{W}_{i+1} - \vec{W}_i) - \epsilon_{i+1/2}^{(4)}(\vec{W}_{i+2} - 3\vec{W}_{i+1} + 3\vec{W}_i - \vec{W}_{i-1})\right]$$

- The first bracket is the 2nd order term
- The second is the 4th order term
- The formula is written for the i direction and must also be applied in j

## 6. Scaling of dissipation terms

### R15 – Selective extinction
- The 4th order term must be cut off in shocks
- The 2nd order term must be cut off in smooth zones

### R16 – Coefficients
$$\epsilon_{i+1/2}^{(2)} = k^{(2)} \max(\Upsilon_i, \Upsilon_{i+1})$$

$$\epsilon_{i+1/2}^{(4)} = \max[0, k^{(4)} - \epsilon_{i+1/2}^{(2)}]$$

with

$$\Upsilon_i = \frac{|p_{i+1} - 2p_i + p_{i-1}|}{p_{i+1} + 2p_i + p_{i-1}}$$

and the given constants:
- $k^{(2)} = 1/2$
- $1/128 \leq k^{(4)} \leq 1/64$

## 7. Scalar dissipation factor (spectral radius)

### R17 – Dissipation multiplied by a scalar
The dissipation is multiplied by a scalar value: the spectral radius $\hat{\Lambda}$.

The spectral radius is the largest eigenvalue of the Jacobian (maximum information speed).

### R18 – Formula for i-face and j-face
$$\hat{\Lambda}_c^I = (|\vec{v} \cdot \vec{n}_I| + c) \Delta S_I$$

$$\hat{\Lambda}_c^J = (|\vec{v} \cdot \vec{n}_J| + c) \Delta S_J$$

where $\vec{v} \cdot \vec{n}$ is the contravariant velocity (scalar).

### R19 – Value on the face
$$\hat{\Lambda}_{i+1/2}^S = \frac{1}{2}(\hat{\Lambda}_c^I)_i + \frac{1}{2}(\hat{\Lambda}_c^J)_{i+1/2}$$

(the slide shows the sum of I and J contributions on the face).

## 8. Calculation of spectral radius at cell center

### R20 – Basic data
The surface normal and area are face data (already calculated in "computation of mesh metrics").

### R21 – Average to center
To obtain the spectral radius at the cell center, the values of the two faces bounding the cell in the I, J (and K if present) directions must be averaged.

## 9. Boundary conditions

### 9.1 No-Slip Wall Boundary Condition

#### R22 – Ghost velocity (no-slip)
For ghost cell 1 (the one just behind the wall), the velocity must be imposed by normal reflection:

$$\vec{v}_1 = \vec{v}_2 - 2V_2 \hat{n}$$

with

$$V_2 = u_2 n_x + v_2 n_y + w_2 n_z$$

i.e., remove twice the normal component of the velocity from the interior point.

#### R23 – Pressure and density copied
The pressure and density values of the ghost layers are simply copied from the interior cell (denoted "2" in the diagram):

$$P_1 = P_2$$
$$P_0 = P_2$$
$$\rho_1 = \rho_2$$
$$\rho_0 = \rho_2$$

(indices 0 and 1 are the two layers on the wall side; 2 is the first interior cell)

### 9.2 Farfield

Four cases are distinguished, as shown on the slide.

#### R24 – Supersonic inflow
$$\vec{W}_b = \vec{W}_a$$

→ the boundary state is directly the freestream state.

#### R25 – Supersonic outflow
$$\vec{W}_b = \vec{W}_d$$

→ the boundary state is directly the interior state.

#### R26 – Subsonic inflow
Use the formulas given on the slide (mixture of interior state $(\rho_0, c_0)$ and freestream state "a") to reconstruct $\rho, u, v, w, p$ on the boundary.

The text specifies:
- $\rho_0$ and $c_0$ are taken from the interior point
- "a" values come from the freestream

#### R27 – Subsonic outflow
Same principle as for subsonic inflow but with the formulas at the bottom of the slide (imposed pressure + extrapolation of velocity from the interior).

#### R28 – Ghost cells filling
"Values are then extrapolated from the interior and the BC to the ghost cell centers."

→ after obtaining the state on the boundary, these values must be extended to the centers of ghost cells.

## 10. Time integration

### R29 – Basic scheme
The time scheme used is explicit Euler:

$$\Omega_i \frac{\partial \vec{W}_i}{\partial t} + \vec{R}_i = 0$$

### R30 – Time discretization
At iteration $n \to n+1$:

$$\Omega_i \frac{\vec{W}_i^{n+1} - \vec{W}_i^n}{\Delta t_i} + \vec{R}_i^n = 0$$

hence the increment:

$$\Delta \vec{W}_i = \vec{W}_i^{n+1} - \vec{W}_i^n = -\frac{\vec{R}_i^n \Delta t_i}{\Omega_i}$$

### R31 – Local time step
The time step per cell is:

$$\Delta t_i = \text{CFL} \frac{\Omega_i}{(\hat{\Lambda}_c^I + \hat{\Lambda}_c^J + \hat{\Lambda}_c^K)_i}$$

### R32 – Spectral radii
The spectral radii in each direction are those already defined:

$$\hat{\Lambda}_c^I = (|\vec{v} \cdot \vec{n}_I| + c) \Delta S_I$$
$$\hat{\Lambda}_c^J = (|\vec{v} \cdot \vec{n}_J| + c) \Delta S_J$$
$$\hat{\Lambda}_c^K = (|\vec{v} \cdot \vec{n}_K| + c) \Delta S_K$$
